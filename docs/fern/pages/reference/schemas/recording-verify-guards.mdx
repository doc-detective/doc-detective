Structural assertions on the produced video file, evaluated when the recording stops. Unlike checkpoint drift (a WARNING), a violated guard FAILs the stopRecord step — these are properties you explicitly demand of the artifact.

## Referenced In

- [Record (detailed)](/reference/schemas/record-detailed)

## Fields

Field | Type | Description | Default
:-- | :-- | :-- | :--
minDuration | number | Optional. Fail if the video is shorter than this many seconds.<br/><br/>Minimum: 0 | —
maxDuration | number | Optional. Fail if the video is longer than this many seconds.<br/><br/>Minimum: 0 | —
resolution | one of:<br/>- boolean<br/>- object([Verify resolution (detailed)](/reference/schemas/verify-resolution-detailed)) | Optional. Verify the video's dimensions (±2 pixels — encoders round to even dimensions). `true` compares against the capture plan's resolved expectation (the crop rectangle when a window/viewport crop applied, else the capture frame size; skipped when no expectation exists for the engine). An object compares literal dimensions. | —
notBlack | boolean | Optional. Fail if the video is essentially all black (black frames cover 95% or more of its duration) — the classic symptom of a broken screen capture. | —

## Examples

{
  "minDuration": 42,
  "maxDuration": 42,
  "resolution": true,
